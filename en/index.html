A plainspoken guide to money and Bitcoin

Understand money first.

“All great technologies begin at the margins.”

Hukanbit (胡侃比特 — roughly, “shooting the breeze about Bitcoin”) is a Chinese essay series with one goal: that anyone, with no finance background at all, can understand how Bitcoin works, what it is worth, and why it matters to ordinary people.

There are no charts here, no price predictions, and no “last chance to get in.” If you remember only one thing after reading, I hope it is this: before you spend money or save money, understand money.

The English edition is young — the first essay is translated below, and the rest are on their way. The full museum (interactive lab, gallery, library) currently speaks Chinese; you are warmly invited to wander it anyway.

About

I write in a deliberately casual voice — wandering wherever the thought goes — because the ideas themselves are heavy enough. The Chinese museum behind this page includes an interactive lab (mine a block, race a double-spend, feel the mempool), a gallery of monies, a reading room, and a treasury hall.

One more thing, about the blueprint: what you see today is a foundation. This museum is still under construction — a newsroom, a teahouse for readers, and a payments window are already drawn on the plans — and the payments drill room just opened (Chinese first). One room at a time.
